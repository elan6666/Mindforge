# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mindforge serves builders, researchers, writers, and power users who want an AI workspace that behaves like a configurable team rather than a single chat box. They work across codebases, documents, research tasks, model providers, local Skills, MCP tools, uploaded files, approvals, and exported work products.

## Product Purpose

Mindforge coordinates multi-agent work from task intake through evidence gathering, model routing, review, approval, and artifact export. Success means users can understand what happened, why it happened, which models and tools were used, and where the finished outputs live.

## Brand Personality

Professional, controllable, traceable. The product should feel like a serious AI operations desk: calm enough for repeated daily work, explicit enough for audit and review, and sharp enough to make agent orchestration feel like a real product advantage.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic chat wrappers, decorative AI-gradient dashboards, shallow Kimi-style category launchers, opaque one-shot agent outputs, and crowded feature grids that hide the workflow. Do not make Mindforge feel like a toy website generator, a terminal-only coding agent, or an IDE clone.

## Design Principles

- Make the AI team visible: show role ownership, stages, handoffs, reviews, and open questions.
- Preserve evidence: sources, files, tool calls, model choices, approvals, and artifacts should be easy to inspect.
- Treat workflows as products: Code Forge, Doc Forge, Research Forge, Site Forge, and Data Forge need clear task entry, active team, progress, and outputs.
- Reward control over spectacle: the interface should help users steer, approve, compare, and recover work.
- Convert conversations into assets: final outputs should graduate into Artifact Library and project memory, not disappear inside chat history.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve visible focus states, keyboard-operable controls, reduced-motion alternatives, readable Chinese and English mixed labels, and layouts that remain usable on laptop and narrow desktop widths.
